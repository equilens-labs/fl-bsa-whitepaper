
\section{Evaluation Design}
This whitepaper evaluates three data slices produced by the v5.0.0 release-evidence pipeline: (i) the historical baseline (input dataset), (ii) the amplification (bias-preserving) synthetic slice, and (iii) the intrinsic (de-biased) synthetic slice with deterministic post-labelling when enabled. We report selection-rate fairness (disparity ratios and approval-rate gaps) using aggregated counts with deterministic uncertainty. Calibration requires probability scores and calibration bins and is out of scope for this scenario.

This fixture is used as a positive control: the product generation path supports controlled bias injection, and the intake for this run carries a known gender AIR below the four-fifths threshold. A below-threshold AIR in the historical and amplification slices is therefore the detection target, not a product failure. The interpretive question is whether the amplification (bias-preserving) branch preserves that signal while the intrinsic (de-biased) branch removes decision-label disparity under its post-label policy. This framing is limited to the synthetic fixture and the configured gender pair in this evidence bundle.
