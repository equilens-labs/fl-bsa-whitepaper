\begin{abstract}
We present a review-oriented, statistically rigorous evaluation of Equilens' Fair Lending Bias-Simulation Appliance (FL-BSA), a self-hosted fair-outcomes evidence appliance for regulated credit decisions. The paper formalises core selection-rate fairness estimands---including the Adverse Impact Ratio (AIR) and approval-rate gaps---and reports deterministic uncertainty using Wilson confidence intervals for group rates and a delta-method confidence interval for AIR. We describe the data-privacy and provenance boundaries, and demonstrate the framework on a synthetic fair-lending audit scenario, surfacing threshold exceptions when present with quantified uncertainty suitable for governance and regulatory review. Calibration is out of scope for this scenario.
\end{abstract}
